Geriartic Support and Care

Geriatric care extends beyond hospital care and is a wider canvas as can be seen in some of the developed countries. Our website is committed to addressing the unique needs of seniors across the country, recognizing the diverse cultural and socio-economic landscape.

Population Statistics of Elderly in India

  • As India moves closer to become the most populous country in the next seven years, the country is facing serious concern about the ageing population.

  • The elderly population has its own vulnerabilities based on education, socioeconomic condition, gender, place of residence.

  • Both the proportion and size of older adults population is increasing over time.India is undergoing a demographic transition from a population with high fertility, high birth rates, and higher death rates to a population with low fertility, low birth, and death rates As this happens, we see a steady increase in population of elderly in India with 8.6% of the population above age of 60 years as per 2011 census which is projected to go over 10% by 2020.

  • The decadal growth rate of elderly population between 2001 and 2011 census has increased for the first time since independence. Also, the absolute number of elderly women has outnumbered elderly men in the last two decades.

  • Our elderly population in India has high rates of illiteracy (56%) as per census 2011. They are economically dependent partially or fully on others (65%) for day to day maintenance. Within this vulnerable group, elderly women and rural elderly are especially vulnerable.

  • This vulnerability is also seen in the form of elder abuse. As per a report based on a survey of urban elderly from 23 cities, 25% of elderly confirm being victim of one form of abuse or other.

  • Global Burden of Diseases 2017 estimated a total of 990,9501 deaths in India. Around 59% of them were estimated to be in elderly population. It estimates cardiovascular diseases, chronic respiratory diseases, stroke and cancer as the top most diseases causing death in elderly population.
